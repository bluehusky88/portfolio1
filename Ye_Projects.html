Scratch Project

Course: Computer Science Principles

The first project in CSP. A partner and I had to program a game in Scratch. This game had to include levels and some form of increasing difficulty.

While working on this project, I experienced game development for the first time. From this project, I learned that no game is truly perfect. There will almost always be a way to improve and make it better. I also learned that "bug fixes" are very easily taken for granted. Trying to pinpoint the root of a problem and fix it is far more difficult than I originally percieved it to be.

MIT App Inventor Project

Course: Computer Science Principles

In the MIT App Inventor project, a partner and I were tasked with making a functional app with the MIT app inventor software.

While working on this project, I learned that developing an app, especially on android, is very difficult. My partner and I had to test the app multiple times to ensure that it was working properly. Every time we changed or added a feature, there was a chance that the app would completely break. We would then restore a working version and try to figure out where we went wrong.
